#Chemistry 
$$E = E^{0} - \frac{RT}{nF}lnQ$$
$$aA+bB \leftrightarrow cC+dD$$
At $25C$ ($298K$) it becomes:
$$E = E^0 - \frac{0.0592}{n}lnQ$$
Where:
1. $E =$ Potential at Nonstandard Conditions
2. $E^0 =$ Standard Electric Potential
3. $R =$ Gas Constant, $8.314\,J/molK$
4. $T=$ Absolute Temperature
5. $n=$ Moles of electrons transferred
6. $F=$ Faraday's constant, $96500 C/mol$
7. $Q=$ Reaction Quotient
	1. Thermodynamic variable
	2. Stability of Reversible Reactions
	3. $Q = \frac{[C]^c[D]^d}{[A]^a[B]^b}$
		1. Different from Equilibrium constant
	4. Concentrations are time dependent
$$E = E^0 - \frac{RT}{nF}ln\frac{[C]^c[D]^d}{[A]^a[B]^b}$$
